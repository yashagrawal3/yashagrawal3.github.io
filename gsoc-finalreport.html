GSoC Final Report

This blog includes my work summary and my experience during Google Summer of Code’18 with Sugar Labs.

I am in the final phase of completing my project “Say no to Gtk2”, which includes The Porting of a few popular activities of sugarlabs from Gtk2 and GST0.10 to Gtk3 and GST1.0. It also included porting all activities to current standards followed by Sugar Labs to keep proper maintenance track of the activity. All students will now be able to use these activities again.

GSoC Experience

It has been a great journey till here with Sugar Labs. I started to contribute to Sugar Labs in December 2017 and started making pull requests and taking part in weekly meetings. I started with sugarlabs website which involved CSS and js work, it was pretty comfortable with and then slowly moved towards working on sugar activities. I was completely alien to Gtk+ and Desktop Client development then but started getting hang of it a few days later. I have a total of 98 commits and 70 pull requests merged till date in Sugar Labs, a majority of them being Port to Gtk+3. I gained experience and some expertise in libraries like Gtk+, GStreamer, Cairo, Pango, Drawable etc.

Working with Sugar Labs

Sugar Labs is a great organization with some very talented developers, you always learn from others so much. A big shout out to Chihurumnaya Ibiam for being such a great mentor, it was a great experience working with him. James Cameron(quozl) has been a great mentor throughout, he has been kind enough take out time to explain me everything from scratch with great detail about things I had absolutely no idea about. Walter Bender has always been there for help and support. I would love to stay connected to Sugar Labs and keep on contributing to Sugar Labs
